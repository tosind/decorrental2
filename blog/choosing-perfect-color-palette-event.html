How to Choose the Perfect Color Palette for Your Event or Wedding

Published February 2026 | Ottawa Decor Rentals

One of the most powerful decisions you'll make when planning your event is choosing your colour palette. The right combination of colours sets the mood, guides every purchasing and rental decision, and creates the visual harmony that makes a celebration feel intentional and beautiful.

Start With Your Venue

Before settling on a palette, spend time in your venue and note the permanent fixtures: wall colours, flooring, draping, wood tones, and natural light. A palette that fights your venue will always look off. Choose colours that complement and enhance what's already there.

Consider the Season

  • Spring: Blush pinks, lavender, sage green, and soft whites feel fresh and romantic.
  • Summer: Bright corals, tropical greens, sky blue, and sunny yellows bring warmth and energy.
  • Fall: Burnt orange, deep burgundy, gold, and earthy terracotta feel cozy and dramatic.
  • Winter: Deep navy, emerald green, icy silver, and rich burgundy create a moody, luxe atmosphere.

The Classic Rule: 60-30-10

Interior designers use this rule and it works beautifully for events. Choose a dominant colour (60%) for your main surfaces like tablecloths and walls, a secondary colour (30%) for accents like florals and chair sashes, and a pop colour (10%) for small details like candle holders or ribbon.

Stick to 2–3 Colours Maximum

Restraint is the key to looking polished. The most elegant events rarely have more than two or three intentional colours running through them. Add variety through texture and scale — a velvet navy table runner next to a satin navy tablecloth adds richness without adding more colour.

Test Your Palette in Context

Pull physical fabric swatches or paint chips and view them next to each other in the light of your venue. Colours look different in different lighting — a warm amber candlelit reception will read differently than a bright daylight brunch.
